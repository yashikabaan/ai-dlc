# Scrum Alignment Rules

Last Updated: `<YYYY-MM-DD>`

## Product Backlog
- Owned by: Product Owner
- AI Support: Product AI and Planning AI for structuring and clarity
- Human Responsibility: priority, scope, and acceptance definition

## Sprint Backlog
- Owned by: Delivery team
- AI Support: Planning AI for decomposition and dependency visibility
- Human Responsibility: final sprint commitment

## Refinement
- Purpose: improve story clarity and readiness
- AI Support: draft acceptance criteria, identify gaps and risks
- Human Responsibility: confirm fit, feasibility, and value

## Sprint Planning
- Purpose: select work based on capacity and priority
- AI Support: effort hints, dependency checks, risk flags
- Human Responsibility: commitment and sequencing decisions

## Daily Meeting
- Purpose: progress sync, blockers, near-term coordination
- AI Support: summarize updates and blocker patterns
- Human Responsibility: decisions on reassignment/escalation

## Weekly Progress Check
- Purpose: inspect outcomes vs plan
- AI Support: variance summary and trend highlights
- Human Responsibility: corrective actions

## Review
- Purpose: verify value delivered and collect stakeholder feedback
- AI Support: summarize completed scope and evidence
- Human Responsibility: acceptance/rejection and next priorities

## Retrospective
- Purpose: improve team process and quality
- AI Support: pattern extraction from updates/incidents
- Human Responsibility: choose and own improvements

## Human vs AI Responsibility Principle
- AI supports analysis, drafting, and consistency.
- Humans own final product, technical, and process decisions.
